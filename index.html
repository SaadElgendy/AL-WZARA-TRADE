AL-WZARA TRADE

Day trading is speculation in security securities, specifically the buying and selling of financial instruments during the trading day. All trades are closed before the market closes on the trading day. Therefore, traders who trade these trades with the motive of profit are considered speculators. Fast trading methods contrast with long-term trades that support buy-and-hold and value investing strategies. Day traders exit trades before the market closes to avoid uncontrollable risks and negative price gaps between the close on a given day and the next day's price at the open.

Day traders generally use a marginal leverage ratio. In the US, Guideline T allows a maximum initial leverage ratio of 2:1, but many brokers allow a leverage ratio of 4:1 as long as this ratio is reduced to 2:1 or less by the end of the trading day. In the United States, people who trade more than four days each week are called typical day traders, and are required to hold $25,000 worth of stocks in their accounts. Since margin interest is only earned on overnight balances, the trader may not pay any margin interest fees, although the risk of a margin call still exists. Marginal interest rates usually depend on the loan interest rate requested by the broker.
